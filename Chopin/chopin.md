## 📖 Key Takeaways: Childhood & Foundations (Doc 1)

* **Family & Roots:** Born to a Polish mother and a French father who worked as a successful French professor. He grew up with three sisters; his sister Ludwika was his very first piano teacher when he was 4 years old.
* **Unorthodox Education:** Chopin only ever had *one* formal piano teacher (Wojciech Żywny). After that, his father supported him to completely teach himself how to play, focusing instead on formal composition lessons. 
* **The Polish Mozart:** By age 8, he was a famous prodigy performing for high society. While Vienna had Franz Liszt, Poland proudly claimed Chopin as their own equal.
* **Emotional Anchors:** He was deeply connected to his close friends and family. His sister Ludwika shared a deep spiritual bond with him and was ultimately right next to him at his deathbed.

---

## 🎼 Musical Technique & Exile (Doc 2)

* **The Singing Piano:** Chopin was heavily influenced by German and Italian music (but not French music). He tried to mirror Italian opera singing on the piano, treating the right hand like a high "soprano" voice. He believed piano playing should feel like natural breathing.
* **The Political Fugitive:** Due to a revolution back home, Chopin became an exile and was banned from ever entering Poland again. This permanent homesickness is why he used intense Polish rhythms and sounds in his music.
* **Life and Lovers:** He had various lovers throughout his life. One particularly deep relationship involved a picture that was cut in half, where he kept one part and she owned the other.
